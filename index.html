FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Get instant answers to common HVAC cost questions from certified experts.

Our estimates are highly accurate because we use real-time utility rate data from EIA and state energy offices, equipment efficiency ratings from AHRI certified databases, and formula methodology validated by licensed mechanical engineers. For a typical 2,000 sq ft home, our estimates fall within 10-15% of actual costs. Actual costs vary based on your specific usage patterns, local climate, and equipment condition. We recommend using our calculators as a budgeting tool and getting 2-3 contractor quotes for installation projects.

SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) measures AC efficiency over an entire cooling season — higher SEER means lower operating costs. EER (Energy Efficiency Ratio) measures efficiency at peak outdoor temperature (95°F) — important for hot climates. HSPF (Heating Seasonal Performance Factor) measures heat pump heating efficiency — higher HSPF means cheaper heating. Minimum federal standard is SEER 14 for new systems, but ENERGY STAR models start at SEER 15-16. Cold-climate heat pumps should have HSPF 9-10+.

Furnaces typically last 15-20 years. At 15 years, consider replacement if: your furnace is below 80% AFUE (modern units are 90-98% efficient), you spend more than $150/month on heating bills, you hear unusual noises or experience frequent breakdowns, or your home has uneven heating. A new 95% AFUE furnace can save 15-25% on heating costs compared to an 80% AFUE unit. The EPA recommends replacement when efficiency drops below 80% AFUE due to environmental impact.

It depends on your climate. In mild climates (Southern US, coastal areas), heat pumps save $400-800/year because they're 2-4x more efficient than electric resistance heating. In cold climates (Northern US, Canada), gas furnaces often cost less to operate unless you install a cold-climate heat pump (HSPF 9-10+). Our Heat Pump Cost Calculator compares exact costs for your location. Consider heat pumps even in cold climates if you lack gas service — they're cleaner and increasingly cost-competitive.

Wrong sizing is the most expensive HVAC mistake. Undersized systems run constantly without reaching temperature. Oversized systems short-cycle, waste energy, and fail prematurely. Proper sizing requires a Manual J load calculation that accounts for: home square footage, insulation R-values, window U-factors, duct leakage, local climate data (heating/cooling degree days), and sun exposure. A professional Manual J takes 30-60 minutes. Never size based on "rules of thumb" like 1 ton per 500 sq ft — this outdated method leads to wrong sizing in 80% of homes.

Annual maintenance costs $150-300/year for professional tune-ups (spring for AC, fall for furnace). This investment prevents $1,200-2,500 in emergency repairs over 5 years according to our field data. DIY maintenance (cleaning filters monthly, keeping outdoor units clear) is free. Budget $150-280 annually for a professional service plan covering both heating and cooling systems. Skip maintenance only if you're comfortable paying $1,500-3,500 for compressor replacement when a $150 tune-up could have prevented it.

Smart thermostats save $130-180/year on average, with payback periods of 1.5-3 years ($150-250 unit + $50-100 installation). However, savings depend on your current behavior: if you already religiously set your thermostat to a schedule, you save only 3-7%. If you never adjust it, you save 12-17%. Savings are highest with heat pumps (10-15% of heating costs) because they run most consistently. Choose ENERGY STAR certified models with geofencing and learning algorithms for maximum efficiency.
