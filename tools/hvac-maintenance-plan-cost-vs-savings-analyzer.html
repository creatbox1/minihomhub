Are HVAC Maintenance Plans Worth It?

HVAC maintenance plans typically cost $150-$350 per year and include two tune-ups (spring and fall), priority service, and a discount on repairs. Whether they save you money depends on your system's age, type, and reliability. The U.S. Department of Energy's HVAC maintenance guide emphasizes that regular professional maintenance is key to system longevity and efficiency.

What's Usually Included

  • Spring AC tune-up (clean coils, check refrigerant, inspect electrical)
  • Fall furnace/heating tune-up (check heat exchanger, clean burners, test safety)
  • Priority scheduling for emergency repairs
  • 10-20% discount on repair labor and parts
  • No overtime charges or diagnostic fees

💡 Pro Tip: The biggest financial value of a maintenance plan isn't the tune-ups — it's the avoided major repairs. Properly maintained systems are 50-70% less likely to fail during peak season. A $200 plan that prevents one $800 emergency repair saves you $600. See ENERGY STAR's HVAC maintenance checklist for recommended seasonal tasks.
