How to Decide: Repair vs Replace

When your HVAC system breaks down, you face a stressful decision: pay for the repair or invest in a replacement. Two industry-standard rules help make this decision data-driven rather than emotional.

The $5,000 Rule

Multiply the age of your system (in years) by the estimated repair expense. If the result is over $5,000, replacement is usually the better choice. Example: a 12-year-old system with a $1,200 repair = $14,400 → replace. A 5-year-old system with a $400 repair = $2,000 → repair.

The 50% Rule

If the estimated repair expense exceeds 50% of the replacement cost, replace instead. Example: $2,500 repair on an $8,000 replacement = 31% → repair. $4,500 repair on an $8,000 replacement = 56% → replace.

Long-Term Cost Projection

Beyond the rules, this calculator projects 5-year and 10-year costs for both paths. The repair path assumes one additional major repair within 5 years for older systems. The replacement path factors in energy savings from higher efficiency equipment. This long-term view often reveals that a seemingly expensive replacement pays for itself within 3-7 years through energy savings alone. Our guide to signs your HVAC needs replacement can help clarify the decision.

💡 Pro Tip: If your system is over 15 years old and needs a major repair (compressor, heat exchanger, coil), replacement almost always makes more financial sense. The U.S. Department of Energy provides guidance on energy-efficient heating and cooling upgrades. The exception is if you plan to move within 2 years — in that case, repair may be adequate.

⚠️ Common Mistake: Only looking at today's estimated repair expense. A $500 repair on a 20-year-old system seems cheap, but that system already costs 30-50% more to run than a new one. The "cheap repair" actually costs you more every month it runs. Always factor in energy waste, not just repair bills.
