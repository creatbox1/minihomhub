Understanding Home Electrification

Home electrification replaces fossil-fuel appliances with high-efficiency electric alternatives powered by an increasingly clean grid. The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) provides significant tax credits and rebates to make this transition affordable. The ENERGY STAR program certifies qualifying high-efficiency equipment eligible for these incentives.

What's Included in This Plan

  • HVAC: Heat pump replacing gas furnace + AC (or AC-only systems)
  • Water Heating: Heat pump water heater replacing gas or electric tank
  • Cooking: Induction range replacing gas range
  • EV Charging: Level 2 EV charger installation
  • Solar: Optional rooftop solar to offset new electric load

💡 Strategy: Most homeowners should electrify in phases. Start with the HVAC heat pump (biggest energy savings), then water heater, then cooking and EV. Phasing spreads costs and lets you stack tax credits across tax years. Use our annual energy cost calculator to project your new energy costs after electrification.
