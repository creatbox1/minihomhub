What Is a Hybrid (Dual-Fuel) System?

A dual-fuel system pairs an electric heat pump with a gas furnace. The system automatically switches between them at a set outdoor temperature (the switchover or balance point). Above that temperature, the heat pump runs — it's highly efficient in moderate weather. Below it, the gas furnace takes over because heat pumps lose efficiency and capacity in extreme cold. For a detailed breakdown of heat pump installation costs, visit the Heat Pump Cost Calculator.

The Optimal Switchover Temperature

The ideal switchover point depends on your local energy prices and equipment efficiencies. At warmer temperatures, the heat pump's efficiency (HSPF2) makes it cheaper than gas per BTU of heat delivered. As it gets colder, the heat pump's efficiency drops while gas remains constant, eventually crossing over. This calculator finds the exact economic balance point.

💡 Pro Tip: Many thermostats default to 35°F or 40°F as the switchover, but the economic balance point can be as low as 20°F with cheap electricity or as high as 50°F with expensive electricity.
