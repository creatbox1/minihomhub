How to Read These Results

This calculator compares the annual heating operating cost of a gas furnace vs a cold-climate heat pump. It also shows cooling cost comparison between a standard AC and a heat pump in cooling mode. Use the heat pump cost calculator for detailed heat pump installation cost estimates, or the furnace efficiency calculator to analyze gas furnace operating costs by efficiency rating.

Heat Pump Cost Formula

Annual HP Cost = (Heating Load in BTU / HSPF2) / 1000 × Electricity Rate

Gas Furnace Cost Formula

Annual Gas Cost = (Heating Load in BTU / AFUE) / 100,000 × Gas Rate per Therm

Break-Even Electricity Rate

This is the electricity price at which heat pump and gas furnace operating costs are equal. Below this rate, the heat pump is cheaper to run. Above it, gas wins. This helps you understand how sensitive the decision is to local energy prices.

💡 Pro Tip: In regions with electricity rates under $0.12/kWh, a cold-climate heat pump (HSPF2 9.5+) almost always beats gas on operating cost. In the Northeast (high electricity, high gas), it depends on exact rates. Always use your actual utility rate, not estimates.
