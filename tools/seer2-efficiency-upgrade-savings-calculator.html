What Is SEER2?

SEER2 (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio 2) is the updated efficiency standard effective January 2023. It uses the same basic formula as SEER but tests under different external static pressure conditions, making it a more accurate measure of real-world performance. SEER2 ratings are generally 5-10% lower than equivalent SEER ratings. The HVAC Size Calculator can help you determine the proper system tonnage before selecting an efficiency rating.

How Savings Are Calculated

Annual cooling cost = (System Capacity in BTU / SEER2) × Cooling Hours / 1000 × Electricity Rate. For a 2-ton (24,000 BTU) system running 2,200 hours at $0.14/kWh: 14 SEER = $528/yr, 18 SEER = $411/yr — saving $117/yr.

💡 Pro Tip: The best SEER2 value is usually 16-18 SEER2. Beyond 20 SEER2, the diminishing returns are significant — each additional point saves less energy while the equipment cost increases. Only upgrade to 20+ SEER2 if you live in a very hot climate (2,500+ cooling hours) or want the absolute best. The ENERGY STAR heat pump criteria provide guidance on efficiency tiers that qualify for certification.
