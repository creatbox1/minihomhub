Humidity Control & Energy Costs

Humidity dramatically affects how your HVAC system performs and how comfortable your home feels. Adding dedicated humidity control can paradoxically reduce your total energy costs, even though the device itself uses electricity.

How Dehumidifiers Save Energy

In humid climates, your AC must run longer to remove moisture, even when the temperature is already cool enough. A whole-house dehumidifier handles moisture independently, allowing you to raise the thermostat setpoint by 2-4°F while maintaining the same comfort level. Each degree of thermostat adjustment saves about 3-5% on cooling costs. Use the Indoor Air Quality Upgrade Cost & Savings Tool to compare IAQ upgrade options alongside humidity control.

How Humidifiers Affect Heating

In dry winter climates, low humidity makes the air feel colder than it is. Proper humidification (40-50% RH) allows you to lower the thermostat by 1-3°F without noticing the difference, reducing heating costs by 2-8%. According to the U.S. Department of Energy, maintaining proper humidity levels is one of the most cost-effective ways to improve HVAC efficiency and comfort.

💡 Pro Tip: In humid climates, a whole-house dehumidifier typically pays for itself within 2-4 years through reduced AC runtime and fewer compressor repairs. Portable units are cheaper upfront but use more energy per pint of water removed.
