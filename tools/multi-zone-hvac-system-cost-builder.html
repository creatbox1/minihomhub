Multi-Zone HVAC System Types

Ductless Mini-Split Multi-Head: One outdoor unit connected to 2-6 indoor air handlers (wall, ceiling, or floor-mounted). Each zone has independent temperature control. Best for homes without ducts or for additions. Use our ductless mini-split cost and sizing calculator to estimate installation costs.

Zoned Central HVAC: A single central system with motorized dampers in the ductwork. A zone control panel opens/closes dampers based on thermostat demands. Best for homes with existing ducts. See our guide on whole-home zoning system costs and savings for detailed pricing.

💡 Pro Tip: Mini-splits are ideal for 2-4 zones where rooms have different usage patterns. Zoned central is better for 4+ zones in a home with existing ducts. Mixing both (mini-split for additions, zoned central for main) is often the best solution. The U.S. Department of Energy notes that ductless systems can reduce energy losses by 25% or more compared to ducted systems.
