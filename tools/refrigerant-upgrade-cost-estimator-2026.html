2026 Refrigerant Changes — What You Need to Know

As of January 2025, the EPA's AIM Act began phasing down R-410A production by 40%. By 2026-2028, new HVAC systems will use lower-GWP refrigerants: primarily R-32 and R-454B (both A2L classified — mildly flammable but safe when installed correctly). Use the SEER2 Efficiency Upgrade Savings Calculator to evaluate the energy savings potential of a new high-efficiency system using these next-generation refrigerants.

Refrigerant Comparison

  • R-410A (current): GWP 2,088. Being phased out. Existing systems can still be serviced with reclaimed/recycled R-410A, but prices are rising.
  • R-32: GWP 675. Used in mini-splits globally for years. Better efficiency than R-410A. Requires 30% less refrigerant for same capacity.
  • R-454B: GWP 466. Alternative for central systems. Similar performance to R-410A. Used by Carrier, Trane, Lennox.

💡 Pro Tip: If your R-410A system is under 5 years old and has a leak, repair it — R-410A will be available for servicing through 2035+. If it's over 12 years old and has a major leak, replace with an R-32 or R-454B system. The repair is likely to cost more than the prorated value of the aging equipment.

⚠️ Safety Note: A2L refrigerants are mildly flammable (lower than propane). Modern R-32/R-454B systems have multiple safety features — leak sensors, pressure switches, and outdoor installation requirements. Always use certified contractors trained in A2L handling. For official details on the refrigerant transition timeline, visit the EPA's HFC phasedown page.
