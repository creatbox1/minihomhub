Indoor Air Quality Upgrades — What They Cost & Deliver

Indoor air can be 2-5 times more polluted than outdoor air. IAQ upgrades range from simple filter improvements to whole-house ventilation systems. Here's what each upgrade does:

  • MERV 13+ Filter: Captures 90% of airborne particles (pollen, mold spores, pet dander). Minimal energy impact.
  • UV Germicidal Light: Destroys bacteria, viruses, and mold on coils. Prevents efficiency loss from dirty coils.
  • ERV/HRV: Brings in fresh outdoor air while recovering energy. Maintains humidity balance. Can reduce heating/cooling load. See the Humidity Control Cost Impact Calculator for related energy analysis.
  • HEPA Bypass: Filters 99.97% of particles. Best for severe allergies but adds energy cost from fan resistance.

💡 Pro Tip: Start with a MERV 13 filter (cheapest upgrade). If you still have issues, add an ERV for fresh air and a UV light for biological contaminants. A whole-house HEPA is only needed for severe allergies or medical conditions. The EPA's Indoor Air Quality site provides additional guidance on IAQ improvement strategies.
