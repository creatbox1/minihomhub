How Duct Sealing Saves Energy

Your home's ductwork delivers conditioned air from your HVAC system to every room. When ducts leak, heated or cooled air escapes before reaching its destination — wasting the energy used to condition that air. The U.S. Department of Energy estimates that 20-30% of the air moving through duct systems is lost due to leaks, holes, and poor connections. In unconditioned spaces like attics and crawlspaces, this waste is even more significant because the escaping air also creates pressure imbalances that force your system to work harder.

Duct sealing addresses these losses by closing gaps at joints, seams, and connections. Professional duct sealing typically reduces leakage by 70-85%, depending on the location and accessibility of the ducts. The result: more conditioned air reaches your living spaces, your HVAC system runs less, and your energy bills decrease. Duct Loss by Location

  • Attic ducts: Highest loss — attics can reach 140°F in summer and below freezing in winter. Leaks here waste the most energy.
  • Basement ducts: Moderate loss — basements are more temperate but still significantly different from conditioned spaces.
  • Crawlspace ducts: Moderate-high loss — crawlspaces are damp and experience temperature extremes.
  • Conditioned space ducts: Lowest loss — ducts inside your home's thermal envelope lose minimal energy when leaking.

💡 Pro Tip: If your ducts run through an unconditioned attic, sealing them is often the single most impactful energy upgrade you can make — saving $200-$600 per year for a typical home.

Duct Sealing Cost Estimates

Professional duct sealing costs vary by home size and duct accessibility. Typical ranges:

  • Small home (under 1,500 sq ft): $200-$400
  • Medium home (1,500-2,500 sq ft): $300-$600
  • Large home (2,500-4,000 sq ft): $500-$900
  • Very large home (4,000+ sq ft): $700-$1,200

Aerosol-based duct sealing (Aeroseal) costs more ($500-$1,500) but can seal leaks that are inaccessible for manual sealing. Payback for standard professional sealing is typically 1-3 years in homes with significant duct leakage.

⚠️ Common Mistake: Using duct tape for sealing. Standard duct tape degrades within 2-3 years. Use mastic sealant or UL-181-rated foil tape for permanent repairs. Better yet, hire a professional who uses aerosole or mastic sealing methods.
