Our Commitment to Accuracy

HVAC Cost Guide is an independent resource dedicated to helping homeowners in the USA and Canada understand, calculate, and reduce their heating and cooling costs. Every article, calculator, and guide we publish is built on a foundation of verified data, hands-on field experience, and transparent methodology.

For practical tools to estimate your costs, visit our free HVAC cost calculators.

We do not accept payment for positive coverage, nor do we allow advertisers to influence our content. Our recommendations are based solely on what the data and our field experience show will best serve homeowners.

Content Creation Process

Each piece of content follows a rigorous creation pipeline:

1. Research & Data Collection. We gather data from primary government sources including the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), Department of Energy (DOE), EPA Energy Star, Natural Resources Canada (NRCan), and AHRI certified equipment databases. Where applicable, we supplement this with anonymized field data from real home energy audits.

2. Expert Writing. Content is written by Michael Carter, a Certified Energy Auditor (CEA) with 12+ years of residential energy audit experience across the USA and Canada. Every cost figure, savings estimate, and efficiency claim is grounded in either published government data or measured field results.

3. Technical Review. Before publication, all content is reviewed by licensed mechanical engineers and HVAC professionals who verify calculations, data accuracy, and practical applicability. Our reviewers cross-check formulas, confirm that cited sources support the claims made, and flag any assumptions that need clearer explanation.

4. Terminology Check. We ensure all technical terms (SEER, AFUE, HSPF, Manual J, etc.) are clearly defined in plain language so homeowners of any background can understand and act on the information.

Data Standards

All numerical claims on HVAC Cost Guide meet these standards:

  • Sourced and dated. Every cost figure, efficiency rating, and savings estimate includes its source and the date it was last verified. We link directly to primary sources where possible.
  • Field-verified where stated. When we say "based on 340+ home audits," those numbers come from actual before-and-after measurements in real homes, not simulation models.
  • Ranges, not single points. HVAC costs vary by region, home size, climate, and usage. We always present realistic ranges with clear caveats about what affects the numbers.
  • Weather-normalized. All utility bill comparisons use heating and cooling degree day normalization to account for weather variation, ensuring fair comparisons across different years and regions.

Fact-Checking & Review Cycle

We maintain a continuous review cycle for all content:

  • Annual full review: Every article and tool is reviewed at least once per year for data accuracy, broken links, and regulatory changes (e.g., SEER standards, tax credits, rebate programs).
  • Triggered updates: When major energy policy changes, utility rate shifts, or equipment efficiency standard updates occur, affected content is updated within 30 days.
  • User feedback incorporation: When readers identify potential inaccuracies, we investigate and correct within 5 business days, with corrections noted prominently.

Transparency & Disclosure

We believe homeowners deserve to know who is behind the information they rely on:

  • Author bylines: Every article and guide displays the author's name, credentials, and a link to the full author bio page with complete career history and qualifications.
  • Reviewer attribution: Technical reviewers are named and their credentials listed where they have reviewed specific content.
  • No paid placement: We do not accept payment from equipment manufacturers, utility companies, or contractors for inclusion in our guides or calculators.
  • Affiliate disclosure: Any affiliate relationships are disclosed clearly and prominently on the relevant pages. We do not let affiliate considerations affect our recommendations or rankings.
  • Source citations: All external data sources are cited with links to the original material so readers can verify claims independently.

Corrections Policy

If we publish an error, we fix it promptly and transparently:

  • Corrections to factual errors are made within 5 business days of discovery.
  • Each correction is noted at the top or bottom of the affected page with the date and nature of the correction.
  • Significant corrections that change the meaning of an article are documented in a full correction notice.
  • Readers can report potential errors by contacting us at support@minihomehub.online.
