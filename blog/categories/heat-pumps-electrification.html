Whether you are comparing gas furnace vs heat pump costs, evaluating cold-climate performance, or researching 2026 rebate programs, this category brings together everything needed to make an informed electrification decision. Heat pumps are the fastest-growing HVAC segment in North America, and these guides cover installation costs, savings projections, and real-world performance data. For refrigerant change and maintenance topics, visit Refrigerants, Maintenance & Upgrades. To explore smart thermostats and SEER2 upgrades, head to Smart Tech, IAQ & Efficiency.

Homeowners across North America are making the switch to heat pumps at record rates, and my guides cut through the marketing hype with hard numbers. The installation cost guide breaks down costs by system type — air-source, ductless mini-split, and cold-climate — with estimates localized to your state or province. I compare heat pumps head-to-head against gas furnaces using 2026 fuel prices, and the results vary significantly by region: in the Pacific Northwest, heat pumps almost always win on operating cost, while in the Northeast the breakeven depends on your gas and electricity rates. For colder climates, the cold-climate heat pump guide is essential — today's systems maintain efficiency down to -15°F, but sizing and backup heat strategy are critical. The rebates guide catalogs every major incentive program at federal, state, and utility levels; combining the 30% federal tax credit with state rebates can reduce upfront costs by $2,000–$6,000. The ductless mini-split article rounds things out for homes without existing ductwork.
