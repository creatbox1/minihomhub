How to Submit a DMCA Takedown Notice

To be effective, your DMCA takedown notice ("Notice") must be in writing and include the following information, as required by the DMCA (17 U.S.C. § 512(c)):

  1. Physical or Electronic Signature: A physical or electronic signature of a person authorized to act on behalf of the owner of the copyright interest.
  2. Identification of Copyrighted Work: A description of the copyrighted work that you claim has been infringed. If multiple copyrighted works are covered by this Notice, you may provide a representative list of such works.
  3. Identification of Infringing Material: A description of the material that you claim is infringing and where the material that you claim is infringing is located on our website (please provide the exact URL(s)).
  4. Contact Information: Information reasonably sufficient to permit us to contact you, such as a mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
  5. Good Faith Statement: A statement that you have a good faith belief that the disputed use of the copyrighted material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  6. Accuracy Statement: A statement that the information in the Notice is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you are the copyright owner or are authorized to act on behalf of the owner.

Our Response Process

Upon receipt of a valid and complete DMCA Notice, we will:

  1. Review the Notice: We will review your Notice to confirm it meets the requirements of the DMCA.
  2. Remove or Disable Access: If the Notice is valid, we will promptly remove or disable access to the material identified in the Notice.
  3. Notify the User: We will notify the user who posted the content that we have removed or disabled access to the material and provide them with a copy of your Notice.
  4. Counter-Notice: If the user believes the removal was erroneous or the use was fair use, they may submit a counter-notice to us. If we receive a valid counter-notice, we will forward it to you and may restore the material after 10–14 business days unless you notify us that you have filed a court action to restrain the user's activity.
